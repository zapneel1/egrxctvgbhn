# Sourcing Methodology: "Federer Profile" Manufacturing MSMEs

This document details the research, screening, and verification architecture used to identify 25 hidden-gem Indian manufacturing MSMEs mapping perfectly to DeepThought's "Federer Profile".

1. Targeted Sourcing Channels & Boolean Queries
We sidestepped generalized Google queries to minimize noisy, consumer-facing results. Sourcing was funneled through specialized B2B engines, MCA registers, and regulatory logs using targeted Boolean logic:

   B2B/Directory Queries: `site:zaubacorp.com (Pune OR Hyderabad) ("custom synthesis" OR "precision engineering" OR "biologics")`
   LinkedIn Talent Profiling: `(Founder OR "Managing Director" OR CEO) AND (IIT OR UDCT OR ICT OR "PhD" OR CSIR) AND (Manufacturing OR Chemical OR Mechanical)`
   Regulatory/Growth Scans: Cross-referenced target hubs with the official Invest India PLI (Production Linked Incentive) beneficiary listings for automated components and bulk drug parks.

2. Strict Qualification Gates
To make the dataset bulletproof, every target company had to pass three consecutive verification gates:
1.  The Asset Gate: Verified physical factory infrastructure using Google Maps satellite views and State Pollution Control Board (MPCB/TSPCB) operational consent disclosures. This weeded out trading houses and pure software/service agencies.
2.  The Scale Gate: Revenue filtering restricted companies to a strict INR 50 Crore to INR 500 Crore range using credit rating briefs (CRISIL/ICRA) and MCA financial filings.
3.  The Science Gate: Mapped the leadership team's background on LinkedIn to ensure executive decisions are driven by deeply technical founders or PhD-level operators.

3. The Federer Scoring Engine
Companies were ranked using a composite score weighted across three vectors:

 Vector  Weight  Evaluation Criteria 
 :---  :---  :--- 
 Founder Technical Alignment  40%  Academic rigor (IIT/ICT/PhD) and track record of direct patent filing. 
 Niche Boundary/Moat  30%  High-switching costs, custom manufacturing complexity (CDMO, precision tooling). 
 Growth Acceleration Vectors  30%  Recent capital expenditure, PLI selection, or expansion into highly regulated markets (USFDA, Tier-1 OEM). 

Score Cutoff: Only firms achieving a composite score greater than 80/100 were included in the target pipeline.
